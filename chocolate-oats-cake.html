Recipe

Chocolate Oats Cake

Indulge in the rich, decadent delight of our chocolate oats cake. A perfect blend of wholesome oats and luscious chocolate.

Equipment needed

  • Oven |
  • Baking Pan |
  • Spatula |
  • Measuring Cups & Spoons |
  • Whisk Or Electric Mixer |
  • Mixing Bowls |
  • Parchment Paper |
  • Knife |

Ingredients

  • ✦ 1 cup rolled oats
  • ✦ 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ✦ 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ✦ 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ✦ 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ✦ 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • ✦ 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • ✦ 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • ✦ 1/2 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
  • ✦ 2 large eggs
  • ✦ 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Recipe- Let's Get Started!!

  1. Preheat the Oven

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal.

  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, unsweetened cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk until well mixed.

  3. Prepare the Wet Ingredients

    • In another mixing bowl, whisk together the milk, vegetable oil or melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

    • Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Use a spatula or an electric mixer to mix until just combined. Do not overmix.
    • If using, fold in the chocolate chips or chunks and chopped nuts.

  5. Pour into Pan

    • Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, spreading it evenly with a spatula.

  6. Bake the Cake

    • Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs.

  7. Cool the Cake

    • Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a cooling rack to cool completely.

  8. Serve

    • Once the cake is completely cool, dust the top with powdered sugar or cocoa powder if desired. Slice and serve!

    9. Add-On Tips

    • For a richer flavor, consider adding a tablespoon of coffee granules dissolved in a tablespoon of hot water to the wet ingredients.
    • Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or refrigerate for longer shelf life.
